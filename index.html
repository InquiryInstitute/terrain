Automata

Where AI Learns to Move Earth.

Embodied AI Robotics Sandbox

An open research platform where autonomous construction vehicles learn to interact with real terrain — soil, sand, gravel — in a 4×8 ft robotics bed.

We combine AI, robotics, simulation, perception, and real-world physics into a closed-loop learning system. Not a toy: a serious foundation for research, education, and certification.

Why This Matters

Most AI today is disembodied. Language models reason in text but have no physical understanding of the world. Yet intelligence evolved through interaction with matter — digging, moving, building, shaping terrain.

Automata explores: What happens when AI learns by shaping the physical world? AI must eventually understand matter, physics, force, and terrain. Automata is where AI learns how the world pushes back.

System at a Glance

  • Physical terrain environment — 4×8 ft bed, soil/sand/gravel, fiducials
  • Autonomous robots — RC excavators, trucks, dozers; ESP32-S3, micro-ROS
  • Perception system — overhead gantry, RGB/depth, terrain mapping
  • AI planning stack — ROS2 server (Jetson), coordination, deployment
  • Simulation — NVIDIA Isaac Sim for training and sim-to-real transfer
